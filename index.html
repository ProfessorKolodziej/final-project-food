Food culture in Japan

- The traditional cuisine of Japan is based on rice with miso soup and other dishes.

- Japanese cuisine has been influenced by both Chinese cuisine and Western cuisine.

Typical Meals: One-dish Meal

- Popular among people at work

- Curry rice and rice omelet are two representative types

- Usually include rice or noodles with some form of pickle on the side

Typical Meals: Soup and three side dishes

- The foundation of Japanese meal

- Three elements: rice, soup, and side dishes

- Originates from Honzen Ryori

French cuisine

- The French cuisine was influenced a lot by the Italy cuisine. After an Italian woman get married with Henry II Curmantle (1133.3.5-1189.7.6), she had brought the most popular cooking methods of OX-liver, Black truffle, tender steak, and cheese from Italy to France, which became the basics of the French cuisine.

- The materials of French cuisine are mainly focus on beef (boeuf), lamb (agneau), poultry (volaille), seafood (fruits de mer), vegetables (legume), truffle, and caviar…… The heat of cooking is the most significant thing, like the beef and lamb must be medium well

Cuba

Overview of traditional Cuban cuisine:

● Christopher Columbus discovered the island of Cuba on October 28, 1492, claiming it in honor of Spain.

● The colonists resorted to importing black slaves from Africa to operate mines and plantations.

● As a result, both Spanish and African cultures formed the foundation of Cuban cuisine.

Brazil

Overview of traditional Brazil cuisine:

The original indigenous peoples of Brazil, the Portuguese, and the African slaves, brought by the Portuguese, have all had a part in creating this cuisine. The arrival of the Portuguese to Brazil in the sixteenth century brought with them European ingredients and techniques.

Picanha

The hands-down favorite for Brazilians is picanha, a rump cut that's sliced in thin sheets and eaten with rice and beans. For anyone who wants to experience the Brazilian barbecue tradition, picanha is a must.

Moqueca

This traditional soup with tropical fish stew mellowed by slices of plantain and coconut milk and accompanied by the traditional hot sauce called piri-piri and farofa, the toasted cassava-meal accompaniment.

Chile

Overview of traditional Chile cuisine:

● The Spanish came to Chile in 1541 and they brought grapes, olives, walnuts, chestnuts, rice, wheat, citrus fruits, sugar, garlic, and spices. They also brought chicken, beef, sheep, pigs, rabbits, milk, cheeses, and sausages.

● In 1848, many German immigrants came to Chile, bringing rich pastries and cakes with them. Italian and Arab immigrants also settled in Chile, along with other European immigrants. Each group brought its style of cooking to Chile.

Chilean salad or ensalada chilena

Chilean salad or ensalada chilena is a salad containing tomato, onion, coriander and olive oil, and sometimes with chili peppers.

Empanadas De Pino

In Chile, the most traditional empanada filling is called "pino," which is a seasoned mixture of ground beef, onions, raisins and black olives and are topped with hard-boiled eggs.
